## 1. The Meronomy (formal object)

The meronomy is a **bounded distributive part-whole order** carried by vectors in
`[0,1]^D` (the membership chart), generated by two folds from two roots, and
shadowed by a symbolic record store.

- **Carrier.** Per tier, activations `m ∈ [0,1]^D` over a witness basis of
  dimension `D`. Witnesses are independent (no cross-witness norm coupling).
- **Order (exact).** `A ⊑ B  ⟺  m_A ≤ m_B` elementwise. This is the truth
  order; `⊥ = 𝟘 = [0,0,…,0]` (nothing), `⊤ = 𝟙 = [1,1,…,1]` (everything).
- **Order (graded).** `Basis.part` (clipped cosine, `Layers.py:~11472`) remains
  the graded/scored relation used at lookup sites. Exact dominance is the
  semantic ground truth; clipped cosine is its retrieval-time surrogate.
  Subsumption direction is recovered relationally (figure/ground), as today.
- **Two relations, two stores.** *Incidental* parthood is whatever the geometry
  happens to satisfy (decidable by the dominance test). *Asserted* parthood is
  the per-symbol record store (§8). Geometry is registered to the records by
  training (§5); records answer "is", geometry answers "looks like".
- **Copart.** `co(m) = 𝟙 − m`, and relative copart `co_W(A) = m_W ⊙ (𝟙 − m_A)`.
  In the membership chart this means **"the remainder of W"** and nothing more
  (no opposition semantics — see §2). Exact complementation
  (`A ⊔ co(A) = ⊤`, `A ⊓ co(A) = ⊥`) holds **only at Boolean corners**; the
  interior defect is the vagueness/ignorance residue `m ⊙ (𝟙 − m)` (Boole's
  `x(1−x)`), which is a diagnostic, not an error.

## 2. Perceptual Space (PS)

- **Geometry: the positive unit hypercube `[0,1]^D`.** Not a torus: wrapping
  glues `𝟘` to `𝟙` and destroys the order the folds preserve. The torus belongs
  to the signed conceptual chart only (§3).
- **No negation, structurally.** PS is single-channel; every order-reversing
  involution on a chain is conjugate to `1−x`, i.e. affirming. Absence is not
  representable in PS — only remainders are. `part()`'s `max(0,·)` clip is
  inert on PS by construction.
- **Percepts are contiguity-typed.** Receptive-field activations are taken
  log-concave over the witness field; the π fold preserves this class (§4), so
  π-products of percepts are guaranteed single-pointed (convex superlevel
  sets). This is the architectural home of the convexity/śamatha constraint.
- **Code anchor.** `PerceptualSpace` (`Spaces.py:7895`) is π-only per the
  locked Stage-10 decision; this spec is compatible with PS-owns-π and adds
  the weight-parametrization changes of §4 to that layer.

## 3. Conceptual Space (CS)

- **Geometry: per-witness signed arcs; globally a flat torus.** Each witness
  carries one signed coordinate `x ∈ [−1, +1]`: `0` = ignorance, `±1` =
  certainty poles. The symbol is the concatenation of per-witness coordinates;
  per-witness normalization (each witness has its own budget) — **never**
  global spherical normalization, which couples witnesses and penalizes
  independence. Extrinsically (paired `(cos, sin)` slots scaled `1/√D`) this
  is the Clifford torus inside `S^{2D−1}`, so **dot-product lookup remains a
  single matmul** while the intrinsic metric stays flat/product. `embed.py`'s
  wrapped cell (`_wrap_unit_ball`) is the intrinsic chart of this same object;
  the paired-index layout is its extrinsic chart. The CS codebook convention
  (`ConceptualSpace.use_dot_product = True`, magnitude = certainty,
  `Spaces.py:10668`) is retained.
- **Chart map to PS.** `m = (1+x)/2` per witness, order-preserving,
  no cross-talk. Percept injection is `m ↦ x = 2m−1` **restricted to the
  nonnegative face**: unaided perception writes only nonnegative evidence.
  Absence of stimulation lands at `x = 0` (ignorance), **never** at `−1`.
- **Negation (two kinds, two mechanisms).**
  - *Affirming (paryudāsa):* the reflection `x ↦ −x` (equivalently the copart
    `1−m` seen through the chart). Licensed **only in CS** — downstairs the
    same map means "remainder"; upstairs it means "opposite". The
    perceptual→conceptual interface is exactly where remainder is reinterpreted
    as exclusion.
  - *Non-affirming (prasajya):* not an operator but a **write type**: lowering
    commitment magnitude toward `0` without asserting the opposite pole. Its
    only licensed causal sources are (a) rival detection — positive evidence
    for an incompatible raising the negative side (viruddhopalabdhi), and
    (b) gated absence — zero perceptual activation converted to negative
    evidence **only when** a predictor was active and the referent would have
    been perceptible (anupalabdhi gate). No gate ⇒ absence stays at `0`.
- **Truth representation: signed-only (decided).** No stored BOTH. This spec
  adopts and extends the `BivectorRetirementPlan.md` invariant: wires and
  codebooks carry signed scalars (K3); the `[aP,aN]` bivector exists only
  transiently inside `TruthLayer` accumulation and at fold sites.
  - **Fold-time conflict interrupt.** Any σ fold over signed operands that
    would destructively cancel opposite-signed mass on a shared witness must
    not cancel silently: it emits a conflict event (taint/reground/frame-split
    hook) and
  - **per-symbol κ.** accumulates the cancelled magnitude into one scalar
    `κ` (contested-ness) stored with the symbol. κ distinguishes
    "uncertain-because-unevidenced" from "uncertain-because-disputed" at ~1/D
    cost; witness-level conflict detail is recovered by dereferencing to parts.

## 4. π and σ: meronomy-preserving redefinition

Both folds run in the **membership chart** `m ∈ (0,1)^D` (signed CS operands
pass through `m = (1+x)/2` first; results map back). The decided kernels:

- **π (analysis / intersection / part-maker):**
  `π(m) = exp(W·log m + b)` — plain log/exp on memberships, **replacing**
  `PiLayer`'s current odds embedding (`to_mult/from_mult`,
  `Layers.py:3298`). The odds map `(1+x)/(1−x)` is the `p+n=1` (zero-ignorance)
  slice and produces Bayesian sharpening, not contraction; it is retired from
  the meronymic path.
  - **Weight law:** `diag(W) ≥ 1`, `offdiag(W) ≥ 0`, `b ≤ 0`
    (parametrize `W = I + softplus(·)` etc.). Then **contraction is a theorem**:
    `π(m) ≼ m` for all `m`, with operand mixing safe (off-diagonal terms add
    negative log-mass — they can only deepen the part).
  - π preserves log-concavity ⇒ **π makes contiguous parts** (PS guarantee, §2).
  - Exact inverse survives: `m = exp(W⁻¹(log z − b))` with an ε-floor near
    `m = 0`; binary 2D→D folds remain recommender-reversible as today.
- **σ (synthesis / union / whole-maker):** the De Morgan wrap of the same
  kernel: `σ(m) = 𝟙 − π(𝟙 − m)` (probabilistic-sum family). **One fold kernel
  plus one involution implements both operators**; extension
  `σ(m) ≽ m` is the mirrored theorem under the same weight law.
  - σ does **not** preserve unimodality: σ over *overlapping* operands yields a
    connected extent (aggregation); σ over *underlapping* operands
    (`equal(A,B) = 0`) yields a disconnected extent — **abstraction**. The
    classifier `abstract ⟺ σ-composed of underlapping parts ⟺ extent
    disconnected` is decidable and shall be exposed as a relation.
- **Identities and absorbers (the roots do double duty):**
  - π: identity `𝟙` (intersecting with everything is no-op), absorber `𝟘`
    (a single zero witness annihilates — exclusion propagates).
  - σ: identity `𝟘`, absorber `𝟙`.
- **Monotonicity** (`W ≥ 0` + increasing pointwise maps) is retained from the
  current layers; it is what transports the base order up both towers.

## 5. The two towers and their roots

- **σ-tower (extents):** ascends from **`𝟘` and the atom frontier** — wholes
  of atoms; what a symbol *gathers*. Stored in the **extent codebook**.
- **π-tower (intents):** descends from **`𝟙`** — parts of the universe, each
  step an exclusion; what a symbol *requires*. Stored in the **intent
  codebook**.
- **Galois connection.** Extent and intent are anti-monotone adjoints; the
  round-trips `σπ` and `πσ` are closure operators. **Well-formed concepts are
  the fixed points** — symbols stably expressible both as whole-of-atoms and
  as part-of-universe.
- **Registration loss.** `σπσ = σ` (equivalently `πσπ = π`) on stored symbols
  is a differentiable consistency objective that pulls the two codebooks into
  mutual registration; it sits beside CWCE as the meronymic analogue of
  reconstruction error.
- **Towers are within-level order-isomorphic** (monotone folds), so parthood
  computed at the base transports upward; cross-tower queries route through
  shared operands / the base, or through the dominance test on evaluated
  extents. Note depth-1 codebooks store only pure unions / pure
  intersections; natural kinds need alternation `σ(π(·))` across tiers
  (DNF grows on the σ side, CNF on the π side).

## 6. Symbolic codebook in SS: representation proper

- **Location.** The **symbolic codebook lives in `SymbolicSpace`**
  (`Spaces.py:12336`) and is applied to **input arriving from CS** — not to
  input from IS. The IS-side codebook is the **analysis codebook**
  (perceptual quantization): it discretizes stimulation and remains an organ
  of the meronymic space proper. The two codebooks are distinct organs at
  distinct tiers and must never be merged or confused by the plan.
- **Structure: a correspondence, not a set of points.** Each symbolic
  codebook entry maintains a **pair**: the *code-as-written* (the sign — the
  symbol's own vector, qua object) and the *code-for-the-concept* (the CS
  content it stands for). The first is a representation **of** the second and
  is meaningless without **interpretation** — traversal of the stored
  correspondence. Lookup against either side and traversal between sides are
  **distinct operations** and shall be distinct calls: lookup answers "which
  symbol is this?" / "is this concept lexicalized?"; traversal answers "what
  does this sign mean?" (deref) and "how is this concept written?" (ref).
- **Interpretive event.** Two conditions must *both* hold:
  1. **Proximity:** the CS vector lands close enough to a stored
     concept-code (dot-product retrieval per CS conventions);
  2. **Decision:** an explicit interpret-as-word gate fires (policy, not
     geometry — attention/task dependent; the spec requires the gate to be a
     first-class, loggable decision, not an implicit argmax).
  On the event the object is mapped **to an object other than itself**: the
  sign-code is bound in place of (or paired with) the concept-code. This is
  the operation that **increments the epistemic level**: post-interpretation
  the object is a *set of references*, not a bundle of activations.
- **Why this establishes a separate representational space.** Sign-codes and
  concept-codes are both vectors in the same underlying carrier, so the
  representational space exists **within** the meronymic space — yet it is a
  *different* space in virtue of the correspondence: relations among signs
  (qua written codes) are arbitrary with respect to relations among their
  concepts. Geometric `part()`/dominance over sign-codes asserts nothing
  about parthood of referents; meaning flows **only** through traversal of
  the correspondence. This arbitrariness-of-the-sign is the formal content
  of the `R`-cordon (§7): the cordon marks where geometry stops being
  transparent and the correspondence must be consulted.
- **Epistemic level** is thereby defined operationally: level n+1 objects are
  those whose parts are references to level-n objects. Perception is level 0;
  each interpretive pass can raise level by at most 1.

## 7. The Mind subspace (reference-tagged region)

- **Definition.** *Mind* is the subspace of the meronomy whose objects are
  **wholes of references**: every part of a Mind-object is a reference (a
  codebook index / symbol token), not a raw activation pattern.
- **Cordon: one high-order tag bit.** Mind lives inside the same mereological
  carrier (it *is* part of the meronomy) but must be cordoned because its
  parts are read **opaquely** (as pointers) rather than transparently (as
  geometry). Mechanism: a reserved high-order witness/flag `R` per vector
  (or equivalently an index-range partition of the codebook):
  - `R = 1` ⇒ referential object (Mind); `R = 0` ⇒ ground object.
- **Cordon invariants (the plan must enforce these in `Ops`):**
  1. **No transparent folds across the bit.** π/σ over mixed `R` operands is
     illegal without an explicit `deref` (traverse the correspondence
     sign→concept and evaluate the extent, dropping to `R = 0`) or `ref`
     (traverse concept→sign via the symbolic codebook, raising to `R = 1`).
     Both cordon-crossing operations are exactly traversals of the §6
     correspondence — there is no other licensed way across.
  2. **σ-only above the bit.** Within Mind, composition is σ (binding,
     aggregation, listing). π is **delegated downstairs**: to analyze a
     symbol, dereference, run π on the referent in PS/CS, re-symbolize the
     parts. (Symbols share no witnesses qua codes; intersecting codes is
     meaningless — concept combination is non-intersective.)
  3. **part() across the bit is undefined** (return 0 / raise): geometric
     similarity between a pointer and a pattern is a category error.
  4. **Absence lives here.** "not-A" is stored as the *presence* of a
     reference-level marker (a positive token), gated per §3 — never as
     negative mass in PS.
- **Reading.** Objects within Mind are *assumed* wholes-of-references; the
  tag licenses that assumption without inspection. Crossing the cordon is
  exactly the representation event of §6 (upward) or grounding (downward).

## 8. The definitional DAG (intension store)

- **Records.** Per minted symbol: `(operator ∈ {σ, π, atom}, operand IDs,
  tier, κ, R)` — the ramsified per-symbol storage. Sparse; `O(arity)` per
  record. The geometry (both codebooks) is a **cache of evaluated extents for
  minted terms only**; the term space is generative and never enumerated
  (productivity ⇒ no fixed-D geometric embedding of all possible definitions).
- **Shape: doubly-rooted polyhierarchical DAG**, roots `⊤` and the atom
  frontier; **acyclic invariant enforced at minting** (no recursive
  definitions in v1; every chain grounds at atoms). Multiple parents are
  expected (shared subterms); it is a DAG, not a tree.
- **`⊥` is the evaluation sink**, not a record-store node per se: a minted
  term whose constraints annihilate evaluates to `𝟘` — definable-but-empty.
  Evaluate-before-cache is the incoherence detector; `⊥`-valued terms are the
  symbolic-level home of contradiction (cf. κ at the geometric level).
- **Two subsumption engines, one router.** *Analytic*: subterm reachability
  in the DAG (exact, supports inference/vyāpti). *Synthetic*: per-witness
  interval/box dominance on evaluated extents (fast, approximate; the
  taxonomy-as-boxes structure is the cache index, not the taxonomy).
  Containments holding only geometrically are candidate definitions
  (promotable); holding only symbolically are training signal for
  registration (§5).
- **Hyperintensionality is kept**: logically equivalent but distinct
  derivations are distinct records (definition = procedure/history of
  exclusions).

## 9. Out of scope (explicit non-goals for the plan)

- Complex/amplitude (positive-imaginary) encodings of the catuṣkoṭi: explored
  and **rejected** for cost; signed + κ + TruthLayer-internal bivector is the
  decided representation.
- Signed-amplitude "frame-tagged" conflict (the `1−i` orientation degree of
  freedom): deferred; revisit only if WikiOracle frame-contextualized BOTH
  becomes a first-class model citizen.
- Correlational (non-axis-aligned) intents: handle later via a learned mixing
  basis before the dominance test if needed; not in v1.
- Recursive/co-inductive definitions: forbidden in v1 (acyclicity invariant).

## 10. Acceptance criteria (what the plan's tests must show)

1. **Order theorems hold architecturally:** for random admissible weights,
   `π(m) ≼ min(operands)` and `σ(m) ≽ max(operands)` elementwise, on PS and on
   CS-via-chart; monotone transport up both towers.
2. **Roots behave:** `π(A, 𝟙) = A`, `σ(A, 𝟘) = A`, `π(A, 𝟘) = 𝟘`,
   `σ(A, 𝟙) = 𝟙` (to ε).
3. **Contiguity/abstraction split:** π of log-concave operands has convex
   superlevel sets; σ of underlapping operands is flagged `abstract`,
   σ of overlapping operands is not.
4. **De Morgan exactness:** `σ = 𝟙 − π(𝟙 − ·)` to numerical tolerance with a
   single shared kernel.
5. **Registration:** `σπσ = σ` loss decreases and fixed-point set is
   non-degenerate on a toy corpus; asserted records verified by dominance.
6. **Interface laws:** percept injection never writes negative mass; prasajya
   writes occur only via rival or gated-absence paths (unit tests on the gate).
7. **Conflict accounting:** destructive cancellation in σ always emits the
   interrupt and increments κ; `x = 0, κ > 0` distinguishable from
   `x = 0, κ = 0` at the client interface.
8. **Cordon:** mixed-`R` folds raise; deref/ref round-trip preserves extent to
   tolerance; `part()` across `R` is rejected.
9. **DAG invariants:** minting rejects cycles; `⊥`-valued terms are cached as
   empty and queryable as such; analytic vs synthetic subsumption agree on
   asserted pairs after registration.
