## Resolutions

### Step 5 deferral 1 — N-ary `Ops` body wiring on PiLayer / SigmaLayer
**Decision: not doing it.**  PiLayer / SigmaLayer keep their existing
log-domain matmul body.  The learnable logic stays on `sigma` / `pi` /
`not`; `Ops.lift` / `Ops.lower` are pure stateless algebra and don't
own any learnable parameter.  Routing the layers' forward through
`Ops` would break that separation without a corresponding gain.

### Step 5 deferral 2 — reverse `Ops` wiring on PiLayer / SigmaLayer
**Decision: not doing it.**  Same reasoning as deferral 1 — the layer
reverses use the linear-algebraic LDU inverse, not the codebook-search
`Ops.*Reverse`.  Keeping them separate.

### Step 5 deferral 3 — binary rule application strategy
**Decision: option (a) is final.**  Grammar dispatch goes through
explicit two-operand `Ops.lower` / `Ops.lift` calls (the path the
Step 6 cfg dispatcher uses).  Option (b) (pack into N-element tensor +
`forward(binary=True)` STE selection) and option (c) (per-rule
annotation) are not pursued — closely related to deferrals 1 and 2.

### `bind` / `scale` first-class ops
**Decision: not adding them.**  Neither has a clear linguistic role
that the existing `intersection(...)` fallback fails to cover.  The
cfg keeps the fallback form for `PP = intersection(P, NP)` and
`AP = intersection(DEG, AP)`; if linguistic motivation surfaces later,
promote then.

### `# XXX deprecated alias` cleanup on Ops.lift / Ops.lower / liftReverse / lowerReverse
**Decision: warn-only, permanently.**  The four legacy positional
aliases keep their existing `DeprecationWarning` behavior and stay
available indefinitely.  The `# XXX` markers were removed from
[bin/Layers.py](../../bin/Layers.py) and the docstrings updated to
note the warning is permanent (no escalation to FutureWarning /
TypeError, no hard delete).

### `SymbolicSpace.layer` shim removal
**Done.**  Migrated [bin/Models.py:3669](../../bin/Models.py)
(`self.symbolicSpace.layer` → `self.conceptualSpace.pi`) and
[test/test_reasoning.py:283](../../test/test_reasoning.py) (same
migration).  Deleted the `@property layer` shim from
[bin/Spaces.py:6161-6170](../../bin/Spaces.py).  Reframed
[test/test_pi_sigma_ownership.py `TestLayerShim`](../../test/test_pi_sigma_ownership.py)
as `TestLayerShimRemoved` asserting the alias is gone.

### Step 5 verification gap
**Closed.**  Step 6's baseline established 101 passed across the
Step-4/Step-5 unit-test subset (re-verified against a freshly rebuilt
venv).  The original Step 5 numerics were never recorded but are no
longer load-bearing.

### Layer-2 vs Layer-1 split in grammar.cfg
**Decision: drop the split.**  The `[layer2]` section was confusing
without a corresponding runtime distinction (the rule predictor needs
every dispatchable op as an addressable rule, regardless of whether
it's a phrase-structure production or a post-hoc S-op).  Removed
`[layer2]` from the cfg, dropped `Grammar._CFG_SECTION_LAYER2` /
`rules_layer2` from [bin/Language.py](../../bin/Language.py); the
post-hoc S-ops (`true(S)`, `what(S)`, `conjunction(S, S)`, ...) now
sit in `[upward]` alongside the phrase-structure productions, with a
comment line dividing them for readers.

The "Layer 1 / Layer 2" terminology survives in the parent plan
documentation but no longer corresponds to a runtime structure.

### Optional cfg-default flip
**Done as part of Step 6.**  `MentalModel.xml` ships with
`<grammarCfg>data/grammar.cfg</grammarCfg>` and the legacy
`<grammar>` block is gone.  `Grammar.configure(grammar_dict)` still
accepts the dict-shaped legacy form for callers that pass a grammar
in directly (test_grammar_split exercises this).

## What remains genuinely open

Nothing within the scope of the lift / lower / bivector refactor.  The
4 `DeprecationWarning`-emitting legacy aliases (`Ops.lift(left, right)` /
`Ops.lower(left, right)` / `Ops.liftReverse` / `Ops.lowerReverse`) are
intentional and indefinite.

Out-of-scope future work spotted during the refactor:
- The rule predictor's input feature (currently
  `max_depth * pos_dim`) widens linearly with `nPercepts`; revisit if
  `nPercepts` ever climbs into the hundreds.
- `WordSpace.category_codebook` capacity is `max(64, len(categories))`;
  the cfg currently surfaces ~19 categories so the floor of 64 still
  dominates.  No action needed unless the cfg grows beyond ~50
  categories.
- `ChunkLayer` / perceptual chunking lives outside grammar dispatch
  and is unaffected by the refactor.
