## 1. The Meronomy (formal object)

The meronomy is a **bounded distributive part-whole order** carried by vectors
in `[0,1]^D` (the membership chart), generated by two folds from two roots,
and shadowed by a **binary reference table** (§6–§8).

- **Carrier.** Per tier, activations `m ∈ [0,1]^D` over a witness basis of
  dimension `D`. Witnesses are independent (no cross-witness norm coupling).
- **Typing: by relations, not objects.** There is **one global order**, and
  symbols are full citizens of it — real symbols are parts (ink on a page,
  a cell in a table, a token in STM), with genuine form-mereology. What is
  typed is the *relation*, not the *object*:
  - *Incidental* (geometric) relations — dominance, `part()`, overlap — are
    total over everything, including reference cells, and answer **"looks
    like"** at the form level only.
  - *Asserted/semantic* relations live in the reference table (§6–§8) and
    answer **"is"**; they are reached only by table traversal (decode) and
    table search.
  The category error is *reading one relation as the other*, not comparing
  any particular pair of objects. Reference-hood and epistemic order are
  derived from the table; no fold, norm, or dominance test ever sees a tag.
- **Order (exact).** `A ⊑ B  ⟺  m_A ≤ m_B` elementwise. This is the truth
  order; `⊥ = 𝟘 = [0,0,…,0]` (nothing), `⊤ = 𝟙 = [1,1,…,1]` (everything).
- **Order (graded).** `Basis.part` (clipped cosine — `Ops._part_kernel`,
  `Layers.py:11589`, surfaced at `Spaces.py:1388`) remains the graded/scored
  relation used at lookup sites. Exact dominance (`Ops.partOf`,
  `Layers.py:11713`) is the semantic ground truth; clipped cosine is its
  retrieval-time surrogate. Subsumption direction is recovered relationally
  (figure/ground), as today.
- **Two relations, two stores.** *Incidental* parthood is whatever the
  geometry happens to satisfy (decidable by the dominance test). *Asserted*
  parthood is the binary reference table (§8). Geometry is registered to the
  table by training (§5); the table answers "is", geometry answers "looks
  like". This split does double duty as the Mind cordon (§7).
- **Copart.** `co(m) = 𝟙 − m`, and relative copart `co_W(A) = m_W ⊙ (𝟙 − m_A)`.
  In the membership chart this means **"the remainder of W"** and nothing more
  (no opposition semantics — see §2). Exact complementation
  (`A ⊔ co(A) = ⊤`, `A ⊓ co(A) = ⊥`) holds **only at Boolean corners**; the
  interior defect is the vagueness/ignorance residue `m ⊙ (𝟙 − m)` (Boole's
  `x(1−x)`), which is a diagnostic, not an error.

## 2. Perceptual Space (PS)

- **Geometry: the positive unit hypercube `[0,1]^D`.** Not a torus: wrapping
  glues `𝟘` to `𝟙` and destroys the order the folds preserve. The wrapped
  cell belongs to the embedding chart only (§3).
- **No negation, structurally.** PS is single-channel; every order-reversing
  involution on a chain is conjugate to `1−x`, i.e. affirming. Absence is not
  representable in PS — only remainders are. The structural *reason* negation
  lives upstairs is given in §3: only at the reference tier is the sign
  vacant of denotational duty.
- **Contiguity is sequential, not dimensional.** No adjacency is defined over
  the `D` witness dimensions — they are unordered learned features, so
  "neighboring dims", connectivity, and log-concavity over the dim axis are
  not meaningful predicates. Contiguity of percepts is a property of the
  **sequential/derivational axis** and is owned by the existing machinery
  (`Mereology.Contiguous` — derivation-tree back-projection); the
  convexity/śamatha constraint lives there. Consequently σ extents over dims
  are single lumps always (§4), and no geometric abstraction classifier
  exists (§9).
- **Code anchor.** Post the 2026-06-09 Pi/Sigma swap (commit `31e69d1`,
  "Finish PS/SS Analysis/Synthesis refactor"), `PerceptualSpace`
  (`Spaces.py:8038`) owns **σ only** (`self.sigma` — synthesis, the
  bottom-up fold over atoms) and `SymbolicSpace` (`Spaces.py:12661`) owns
  **π only** (`self.pi` — analysis, top-down from unity). This supersedes
  the Stage-10 "PS-owns-π" note in earlier drafts and aligns the hosts with
  §5's roots: the σ-tower ascends in PS, the π-tower descends in SS. At
  cutover these slots are filled by the new `SigmaLayer2` / `PiLayer2`
  classes (§4).

## 3. Conceptual Space (CS): references and their encoding

- **Encoding: gauge-signed embedding × scalar certainty (exact).**
  - *Stored:* a reference row is a unit direction `u`. Its **location** in
    the unit ball is its semantic embedding — similarity structure and
    nothing else. Its stored coordinate **signs are gauge**: the referent is
    always positive content (percepts are nonnegative, §2), so there is
    nothing downstairs for a minus sign to denote. The gauge is fixed at
    mint by orienting `+u` toward agreement with the (positive) referent —
    "the value as stored maps onto the positive percept."
  - *Runtime:* the value of a reference is `a·u` with one scalar
    `a ∈ [−1,+1]` per reference: `|a|` is **certainty** (1 = certain, 0 =
    unknown); `sign(a)` is **polarity** (agrees/disagrees with the
    referent). Truth never lives in stored geometry; it rides the
    activation against it.
  - *Lookup:* pole-quotient retrieval at the reference half — identity by
    `argmax |q·v|`, polarity by `sign(q·v)`; one matmul; certainty survives
    end-to-end since `(a·u)·v = a(u·v)`. `_pole_aligned_score`
    (`embed.py:118`) is the licensed reference-half lookup, and its
    symbol-tier-only restriction now has its reason: quotient lookup is
    correct exactly where stored sign is gauge. Ground/token codebooks keep
    full-vector lookup (entries independent; sign meaningful as form
    content). The current `ConceptualSpace` convention — unit-norm rows by
    EMA, input magnitude as certainty (`Spaces.py:10874–10882`) — is
    **retained as-is**. The wrapped cell (`_wrap_unit_ball`, `embed.py:80`)
    is licensed for embedding position: wrap is harmless where coordinates
    carry no truth.
- **Interface law: injection is factoring, not a chart.**
  - *Percept → reference:* perception communicates two things separately —
    **content selects the row** (embedding match of the positive percept
    against the reference half), and **evidence sets the magnitude**
    `a ∈ [0,+1]`. Absence of stimulation is zero evidence is `a = 0`
    (unknown) — a tautology, not a clamp. The negative half of the scalar
    is **unreachable from the percept path**; its only writers are the two
    prasajya paths below.
  - *Evaluation chart:* `χ(a) = (1+a)/2` maps epistemic scalars to assumed
    memberships when beliefs cash into extents for folds: `+1 ↦ 1`,
    `0 ↦ ½` (Boole's maximal-vagueness point, §1), `−1 ↦ 0`. χ is
    **evaluation, not injection** — no coordinate-wise map from PS
    memberships into a signed cube exists anywhere in the architecture.
  - *Site contract:* percepts cross the corpus-callosum boundary
    (`ConceptualSpace.bind_streams`, `Spaces.py:12255`) **nameless** — no
    naming side-channel; binding to a symbol is search-then-mint-on-
    licensed-miss (§6).
- **Negation (two kinds, one mechanism each).**
  - *Affirming (paryudāsa):* `a ↦ −a`. Licensed only at the reference tier,
    and now derivably so: downstairs the sign is occupied or nonexistent
    (one-sided content); at the reference tier the sign is vacant of
    denotational duty, so it is free to carry polarity. Denial of `A` is a
    **present, certain** signal (`a = −1` on `A`'s row) — never negative
    mass in ground space.
  - *Non-affirming (prasajya):* a **write type**, not an operator: lowering
    `|a|` toward `0` without crossing it. Its only licensed causal sources
    are (a) rival detection — positive evidence for an incompatible raising
    the disagreeing side (viruddhopalabdhi), and (b) gated absence — zero
    perceptual activation converted to negative evidence **only when** a
    predictor was active and the referent would have been perceptible
    (anupalabdhi gate). No gate ⇒ absence stays at `a = 0`.
- **Truth representation: signed-only (decided).** No stored BOTH. The wire
  carries the K3 scalar `a` (the `BivectorRetirementPlan.md` invariant); the
  `[aP,aN]` bivector exists only transiently inside `TruthLayer`
  accumulation and at fold sites.
  - **Truth accounting: measured, not stored (rev 2026-06-10b; supersedes
    the same-day κ / conflict-interrupt / write-type-API formulation,
    which was implemented, reviewed, and withdrawn).** The TruthLayer
    stays the simple catuṣkoṭi accumulator it already is: signed
    idea-vectors (one per TruthSet sentence) weighted by ±trust; no
    per-symbol κ buffer, no conflict events, no write-type API.
    Contested-ness is the **conflict region of the truth measure**: per
    conceptual dimension `k`, the stored signed references split into
    the true/false pole bivector `(T_k, F_k)` (coverage: elementwise
    max over rows of `relu(±row)`; trust is already baked into stored
    magnitudes), and

        luminosity = mean_k [ (T_k − F_k) − min(T_k, F_k) ]  ∈ [−1, 1]

    — total area weighted by sign, minus the regions where the sign
    differs (contradictory evidence; the catuṣkoṭi B corner).
  - **Computed over the codes, no pullback.** Because the §4 weight law
    puts the parthood geometry on the codes themselves, the measure is
    computed directly on stored rows — the previous per-row
    `decode_to_concept` pullback to the mereological ground is
    retired, along with the sequential cumulative fold (which was
    order-dependent). Code-tier areas are an approximation of ground
    areas: exact at mint on constructor pairs, maintained by the
    registration loss (§5), degrading conservatively under mixing
    (over-counting conflict, never losing a true containment).

## 4. π and σ: meronomy-preserving redefinition

Both folds run on **memberships** `m ∈ (0,1)^D` — ground content natively;
epistemic scalars enter only through the evaluation chart χ (§3). The decided
kernels, staged as **new classes** (`PiLayer2`, `SigmaLayer2`); the legacy
`PiLayer` (`Layers.py:3343`, odds embedding `_to_mult`/`_from_mult`
`:3430`/`:3449`) and `SigmaLayer` (`:2553`) remain untouched for
non-meronymic consumers, and the odds map `(1+x)/(1−x)` — the `p+n=1`
(zero-ignorance) slice, Bayesian sharpening rather than contraction — is
retired from the meronymic path.

- **π (analysis / intersection / part-maker), `PiLayer2`:**
  `π(m) = exp(W·log m + b)` — plain log/exp on memberships.
  - **Weight law:** `diag(W) ≥ 1`, `offdiag(W) ≥ 0`, `b ≤ 0`. Then
    **contraction is a theorem**: `π(m) ≼ m` for all `m`, with operand
    mixing safe (off-diagonal terms add negative log-mass — they can only
    deepen the part). For binary `2D→D` folds with operand blocks
    `W = [W_L | W_R]`, the law reads `diag(W_L) ≥ 1` **and**
    `diag(W_R) ≥ 1` per row — this yields the acceptance bound
    `π ≼ min(operands)` (§10.1). The law is the load-bearing member of the
    whole design: it is why a minted whole geometrically dominates its
    parts (§6, §8) — "parent/child is geometrically encoded" is a theorem
    at mint, not a hope.
  - **Implementation delta (code change, not doc tweak).** The law
    conflicts with the current parametrization in three places, which is
    why it ships as a new inner layer (`ContractiveInvertibleLinearLayer`)
    under the new classes rather than a change to existing ones:
    1. `NonNegativeInvertibleLinearLayer` (`Layers.py:1772`) gives
       `d = softplus(raw) > 0` (init ≈ 1) — positive but **not** `≥ 1`.
       New: `d = 1 + softplus(raw)`.
    2. `stable=True` **clamps `d` to `(ε, 1.0]`** (`Layers.py:1822`) — the
       exact opposite regime (the odds chart wanted non-expansion of
       `|x|`). New clamp: `[1, d_max]` — in log-membership space "stable"
       means *bounded amplification* of negative log-mass.
    3. The nonnegative bias path is `b = softplus(·) ≥ 0`
       (`Layers.py:958–964`); the invertible path leaves `b` unconstrained
       (`Layers.py:1851–1853`). New: `b = −softplus(raw)`.
    **Migration is new-class adoption with fresh init** — the kernel swap
    (odds → log-membership) voids trained odds-domain weights anyway;
    legacy classes and checkpoints are never rewritten.
  - Exact inverse survives: `m = exp(W⁻¹(log z − b))` with an ε-floor near
    `m = 0`; binary 2D→D folds remain recommender-reversible as today.
- **σ (synthesis / union / whole-maker), `SigmaLayer2`:** the De Morgan wrap
  of the same kernel: `σ(m) = 𝟙 − π(𝟙 − m)` (probabilistic-sum family).
  **One fold kernel plus one involution implements both operators**;
  extension `σ(m) ≽ m` is the mirrored theorem under the same weight law —
  no separate σ constraint surface.
  - **Single-lump doctrine.** σ does not preserve unimodality, and no
    further classification is made: adjacency is not defined over witness
    dimensions, so "disconnected extent" is meaningless there — **every σ
    extent is a single lump**. The aggregation/abstraction distinction is
    not geometric: class-hood is carried by the reference table (a class is
    a cell — the lasso, §6), and contiguity questions live on the
    sequential/derivational axis (§2). The earlier classifier (declared
    adjacency, `τ`, `ε`-underlap, mint-time abstract bit) is withdrawn
    (§9).
  - **Validity masks (recorded; next pass).** What the withdrawn classifier
    was groping at is *selectional*, not geometric: the C-tier grammar
    wrappers of these folds — `lift()` / `LiftLayer` (`Language.py:2286`)
    and `lower()` / `LowerLayer` (`Language.py:2490`) — are currently
    **total**: they apply over any operand pair, so "colorless green ideas
    sleep furiously" composes as readily as sense does. At some point each
    operator must declare the **mask over which it is a valid operator**;
    outside the mask, application is anomalous composition, not
    part/whole-making. This is deferred to the **grammar-ops corrective
    pass** (the step after this plan); requirement notes are placed in both
    docstrings. Mechanism seeds already in the code: the per-call `gate`
    low-rank operator slicing (`PiLayer.forward`, `Layers.py:3491` →
    `_d_effective`, `:1818`) and GrammarLayer's `tier` /
    `rule_probability` gating (`Layers.py:1933`) — the latter gates which
    rules fire where, not which operands a rule validly takes; the mask is
    the missing third gate.
- **Identities and absorbers (the roots do double duty):**
  - π: identity `𝟙` (intersecting with everything is no-op), absorber `𝟘`
    (a single zero witness annihilates — exclusion propagates).
  - σ: identity `𝟘`, absorber `𝟙`.
- **Monotonicity** (`W ≥ 0` + increasing pointwise maps) is retained; it is
  what transports the base order up both towers.

## 5. The two towers and their roots

- **σ-tower (extents):** ascends from **`𝟘` and the atom frontier** — wholes
  of atoms; what a symbol *gathers*. Stored in the **extent codebook**.
- **π-tower (intents):** descends from **`𝟙`** — parts of the universe, each
  step an exclusion; what a symbol *requires*. Stored in the **intent
  codebook**.
- **The towers ARE the two codebooks (rev 2026-06-11; resolves the
  Stage 8 organ-mapping question — no new organs).** The extent codebook
  **is the PS codebook** and the intent codebook **is the SS codebook**
  — the same two used for incoming IS recognition. Every synthesis
  method shares PS's; every analysis method shares SS's; by Stage 8
  every space carries such a codebook. The consequence is a
  unification, not a convenience: **recognition is tower lookup** —
  matching an incoming IS pattern against the PS codebook just *is*
  matching against cached extents, so perceiving-as and
  instantiating-a-generalization are one operation. All
  non-referential codes in both codebooks are meronymically sorted by
  construction: they are produced from everything/nothing and the two
  folds, so each arrives with a place in the partial order.
- **Galois connection.** Extent and intent are anti-monotone adjoints; the
  round-trips `σπ` and `πσ` are closure operators. **Well-formed concepts are
  the fixed points** — symbols stably expressible both as whole-of-atoms and
  as part-of-universe.
- **Registration loss — the drift-keeper.** `σπσ = σ` (equivalently
  `πσπ = π`) on stored symbols is a differentiable consistency objective
  that pulls the two codebooks into mutual registration; it sits beside CWCE
  as the meronymic analogue of reconstruction error. Under this spec it
  carries a second, load-bearing duty: the table order and the geometric
  order **agree at mint by the §4 theorems**, and training drift is the only
  thing that can un-register them — the registration loss is the mechanism
  that maintains the agreement (§8).
- **Towers are within-level order-isomorphic** (monotone folds), so parthood
  computed at the base transports upward; cross-tower queries route through
  shared operands / the base, or through the dominance test on evaluated
  extents. Note depth-1 codebooks store only pure unions / pure
  intersections; natural kinds need alternation `σ(π(·))` across tiers
  (DNF grows on the σ side, CNF on the π side).
- **Isomorphic pressure: how the corpus sculpts the order (rev
  2026-06-11).** The training corpus reaches the object embedding
  through three channels, all of them order-bearing:
  1. *Composition (the grammatical loop).* Every parsed sentence
     reduces shifted referents into an idea whose
     reconstruction/prediction must succeed; an object code's location
     must make **its compositions** work. This is distributional
     pressure focused through syntax — the reduce traces, not raw
     co-occurrence, are the lens.
  2. *Predication (truth → registration).* Copular and taxonomic
     statements ("an elephant is an animal") become asserted dominance
     pairs, and the registration loss demands geometry agree: **every
     definition read is a constraint pulling one extent above
     another**. Reading is supervising the order. At corpus scale the
     asserted statements accumulate into a large partial order, and
     training drives the geometric order toward **isomorphism** with
     it — the corpus's statements directly sculpt the meronomy, not
     merely the similarity structure.
  3. *Prediction (predict-then-perceive).* Per-word expectations cash
     out through χ into extents; prediction error moves object codes
     so expected referents match arriving ones.
  **The ceiling: all three channels are relational.** They determine
  the embedding only up to structure-preserving relabeling — a Ramsey
  limit: text pins the order, the composition behavior, the taxonomy,
  while the witness basis itself floats (what dimension `k` *means*
  has no exogenous anchor). Direct representation in a **metric input
  space** is what breaks the relabeling freedom: text fixes the
  structure; experience picks the gauge. Some direct experience of
  objects in input space is therefore recommended, and the corpus is
  not purely symbolic even today — dates, magnitudes, coordinates,
  quantities (infobox-style content) are genuine metric fragments,
  carried by the `.where`/`.when` bands; they are the minimum
  available experience channel. (Recorded conjecture: a text-only
  model's "object embedding" is only as good as its corpus's
  predication density — closer to a word embedding wearing object
  structure; multimodal grounding is the missing gauge anchor, and
  multimodal LLMs are trending toward exactly this purchase.)
  **Scoped consequence:** isomorphic pressure applies to the TOWERS
  only. The word↔object table (§6) is insulated by construction —
  symbols are mereologically free atoms — so training cannot bend
  naming toward size: the arbitrariness of the sign survives learning
  because the binding is not a geometric object.

## 6. The unified codebook and the binary reference table

> **Rev 2026-06-11.** This section is restated around the atomicity
> argument. The earlier formulation (one index space with ground and
> reference halves; constructor cells `(op, left, right)` with
> `descend|stop` branch tags; derived epistemic order with a
> `conceptualOrder` mint bound; the binary-branching census) is
> **superseded**: there are two codebooks (the towers, §5), one
> word-keyed binding table, and atomic symbols. No tags, no stored or
> derived order machinery, no constructor cells in the table.

- **Why the binding cannot be geometric (the atomicity argument).**
  Words are meronymically sorted among themselves (form-mereology, on
  input — the SS tower); objects among themselves (the learned semantic
  sort — the PS tower). But for a word to be associated with an object
  in a **truly arbitrary** way, the two **cannot be size-related**:
  any mereological coupling between reference and referent would let
  geometry constrain which words can name which objects, and naming
  would stop being arbitrary. So the link lives in neither tower. It
  requires carriers with no extent at all — **symbols are
  zero-dimensional points**, atoms outside the size order. This is
  Lewis's construction (*Parts of Classes*) executed structurally:
  mereology (the towers) plus atomic singletons (the table's entries)
  yields classes, with no set-theoretic machinery in the geometry. The
  lasso is not a tag and not a cell kind; it is the table row itself.
- **The word/object table.** Rows are **full only** — a row exists iff
  a word is bound to an object (`SS row ↔ PS row`). The table is
  **sorted by word: the word is the key, which makes the word the
  reference for the object.**
  - *deref* (word → object) is the indexed, cheap direction — the §6
    correspondence traversed in its stored order. The serial-mode
    forward shift (§6 above) is exactly this lookup.
  - *ref* (object → word) has **no index, by design** — an object
    stores nothing about its names (naming is neither unique nor
    inherent). Recall is a search of the object side (the recommender
    similarity/dominance probe is the primitive); recognition is
    cheap, recall is search, **tip-of-the-tongue is a failed
    object-side search**. The walk/search asymmetry is thus realized
    as a *sort order*.
  - **There are no half-bindings.** An unbound word is simply an SS
    tower code (adopt-on-first-sight — `adopt_stage0_evidence`,
    `Spaces.py:15724` — is form memory, not naming, and stays); a
    nameless concept is simply a PS tower code. Bound-ness is not
    stored on either side — it is **discovered by querying the table**
    (word side: indexed lookup; object side: search). "Unknown word"
    and "nameless concept" are query outcomes, not stored states.
  - The interpret-as-word **gate** is unchanged: **search, then mint
    (bind) on licensed miss** — a first-class, loggable decision;
    naming follows demonstrated reuse, never first sight. Binding is
    append-only; the census is now simply **table ≤ lexicon**, with
    selectivity enforced by the gate rather than by branching
    arithmetic.
- **Symbols: atomic, eventful, approximately the index.** A symbol is
  created in CS as an **event** — an occurrence with **no `.where` and
  no `.when`** — and is not represented in the model beyond its table
  row; the binding event is the occurrence, the row is the memory.
  For mechanism, symbols use the existing event idiom: a what-code
  that is **"approximately the index"** — an identifier-like,
  quasi-orthogonal code whose duty is identity, not similarity
  (pairwise near-orthogonal points are generically incomparable in
  the dominance order, so symbol codes carry no size relations) —
  plus the standard `+2 where` / `+2 when` bands, **both zero**.
  (Concretely: the `4+2+2` layout — a 4-dim what-code plus the zeroed
  bands, 8 total — is the **MM_20M** configuration, which sets its
  STM value to 8; the widths are per-model config, likely carried
  into other model files, but the architectural content is
  layout-independent: what ≈ index, bands zero.) Realizing the index
  as a code rather than a bare integer is a concession to the loop:
  remembering the meaning of a symbol inside the PS loop becomes an
  ordinary codebook matmul lookup instead of out-of-band index
  plumbing.
- **Wholes of symbols: the taxonomy.** Composition over symbols is NOT
  stored in the table — wholes of symbols are ordinary **σ-tower
  codes** (PS codebook entries gathering symbol-atoms), so the table
  stays mereologically inert and purely binding. Symbols are
  mereologically *free*, yet containable **as atoms** by larger wholes
  through participation in the PS/σ loop (or the grammatical loop) —
  the taxonomy of symbols is built by collection, in the tower.
  Crucially, such wholes **do not exist in space: their `.where` and
  `.when` are zero, unlike any real object** — the zero-band signature
  is what distinguishes symbolic/abstract wholes from percepts, with
  no new wire types. *(Hazard, recorded: if a σ code is driven to `𝟙`
  and saturates, it gathers everything and distinguishes nothing — a
  concept that cannot be thought about. ⊤-saturation is the dual of
  ⊥-annihilation; monitor for it at mint and under training.)*
- **Arbitrariness of the sign, scoped.** Which word a referent gets is
  arbitrary with respect to that referent (embedding position + gauge,
  §3) — the binding, the table row, is the arbitrary/mysterious step,
  and the atomicity argument above is *why it must be*: arbitrariness
  requires size-decoupling, so the binding is carried by an atom, not
  by geometry. Composed structure is transparent (fusion): a minted
  whole's dominance over its parts is real and means bundle
  membership. Form-order and meaning-order remain independent in
  general (§1); they coincide exactly where the §4 theorems force them
  to — on minted tower wholes and their parts.
- **Serial mode: the table in time
  (`<conceptualMode>serial</conceptualMode>`).** The serial/parallel mode
  mutex is the §1 relation split projected onto time. *Parallel* mode is
  the geometry regime: PS runs the whole-slab σ synthesis and SS the
  whole-slab π analysis — transparent folds, incidental order, no symbols
  engaged — and the callosum is the **simple mixing matrix over the `2N`
  stacked slots** (`N` = STM size; the `[2N,N]` `bind_streams` glue),
  fusing the two views per tick. *Serial* mode is the table regime, and
  (rev 2026-06-10c, superseding the earlier LHS/RHS languaging — those
  terms read off a diagram with PS on the left and SS on the right, and
  hid the SS grammar's role) the grammar engages on **both sides as
  grammatical duals**, each governed by its own chart:
  - **SS side: analysis = split + shift.** The serial form of the π
    fold: SS's grammar **splits** symbolic content top-down into
    constituents (binary part-making), identifying constituents by
    **codebook match** (ref-search) instead of the whole-slab π. When a
    constituent reaches a code that is a **word in the SS codebook** (a
    lexicalized symbol), analysis bottoms out there: its **object
    reference is looked up** — a deref through the paired
    `(orth, semantic)` lexicon row, the §6 correspondence traversed in
    its stored (cheap) direction.
  - **The shift is the callosum crossing.** The dereferenced referent is
    placed on the **PS side** — the shift step of a traditional parser,
    once per word, the single licensed crossing. *The word "elephant" is
    a part of the sentence; the object elephant is not*: the sentence is
    a form-whole of word codes (SS side — the sign qua object); the idea
    is a semantic whole of referents (PS side); neither contains parts
    of the other.
  - **PS side: synthesis = reduce + shift.** The serial form of the σ
    fold: PS's grammar **reduces** (binary whole-making) the shifted
    referents into the idea — the parallel Sum becomes a binary Reduce.
    The reduce trace is a bracketing; reduced wholes are tower codes
    (zero-banded when they are wholes of symbols), and naming one is
    the gate's binding (§6 rev 2026-06-11) — so serial mode is how the
    towers' wholes and, at the gate, the table's bindings get written;
    parallel extents are what the codebooks' caches summarize
    (associativity: `n−1` reduces compute the parallel sum's extent
    while leaving the derivation). Bracketings persist only as events
    or via lexicalization — identity is content + participation, §8
    (resolved 2026-06-11). Fold
    ownership (§2) is unchanged — PS-σ reduces, SS-π splits;
    split/reduce are the duals (analysis splits and shifts where
    synthesis combines and shifts); what is moded is the
    binary/derivational form.
  - **Direction swaps the move sets.** The assignment above is the
    forward (comprehension) direction: SS split|shift, PS reduce|shift.
    On the reverse pass (generation) the duals exchange roles — **PS
    split|shift** (σ-reverse is analytic: decompose the idea into
    constituent referents; where a constituent is nameable, **ref-search**
    finds its word — the expensive direction, no object→name storage —
    and the reverse shift places the word code on the SS side) and **SS
    reduce|shift** (π-reverse is synthetic: compose word codes into the
    sentence form-whole). The walk/search asymmetry of §6 is thus the
    cost asymmetry of the two shift directions; tip-of-the-tongue is a
    failed reverse shift, and paraphrase is the fallback
    split-further-until-nameable. Consistent with §2: each fold's
    reverse plays its dual's grammatical role.
  - **Two syntaxes, softly dispatched.** There is an **analytical
    syntax** — the split-side rule inventory, the grammar of
    decomposition — just as there is a **synthetic syntax** — the
    reduce-side inventory, the grammar of combination. The syntaxes
    attach to ROLES, not spaces: the direction swap above moves them
    (SS runs the analytical syntax on forward, PS runs it on reverse).
    Both dispatch as a **soft superposition over their rule
    inventories** — candidate operators weighted by the grammar's rule
    probabilities, candidate constituents/operands by graded codebook
    match — carried within the signal itself (the same principle that
    struck the per-cell κ). Serial mode's discreteness lives only at
    the **commit points** — the single write per tick (the mutex) and
    table writes (minting) — never in the dispatch. The three-way
    shift branch is therefore the READOUT of one soft computation, not
    a switch: a graded lexicon match distributes mass between
    deref-shift (high match) and the ignorance placeholder (the
    unknown-word case is the `a → 0` end of the same lookup), with
    router affinity carrying the marker-word share; segmentation and
    sense ambiguity are the analytical syntax's superposition,
    attachment ambiguity (which bracketing) the synthetic syntax's —
    both entertained until evidence resolves, with no backtracking
    machinery, the bracketing committing only at mint. Mechanism seeds
    already in the code: `rule_probability` gating (which rules fire —
    already continuous), the per-call `gate` low-rank operator
    slicing, and the recommender's priming weights. Whether the two
    inventories are independent or ONE inventory seen through the §4
    De Morgan dual (each synthetic rule sharing its kernel with its
    analytic dual, as SigmaLayer2 shares PiLayer2's kernel) — and
    whether validity masks are declared per rule or per role — is
    deferred to the grammar-ops corrective pass.
  - **Workspace.** The idea stack (`ShortTermMemory`, `Layers.py:9770`;
    capacity 8, newest-at-slot-0, shift/reduce driver inlined) is the
    PS-side subsymbolic workspace — where ideas exist — and is
    **structurally unchanged**: it already holds `[B, 8, concept_dim]`
    semantic content; only the supply changes. The SS-side stack holds
    the symbolic constituents under analysis (word codes at its
    leaves). In serial mode the per-tick `2N` fusion is bypassed — the
    glue's fusion duty migrates into the shift, so referents enter the
    workspace only when grabbed: one-way storage enacted in time. (The
    serial, capacity-limited symbolic workspace over a parallel
    subsymbolic substrate is the intended correspondence with conscious
    processing.)
  - **The shift rule branches three ways.**
    1. *Content word* — deref-shift (above): **use**.
    2. *Closed-class / marker word* — nothing shifts; the word binds to
       the router as an operator marker (the GrammarLayer
       `SurfaceSchema` marker slots), selecting/parameterizing the next
       reduce or split.
    3. *Unknown word* — a word code with no semantic pairing: the
       **search-then-mint gate's natural host is the shift move**;
       unlicensed ⇒ shift an ignorance placeholder (`a = 0`), licensed ⇒
       mint.
    **Mention** falls out as a shift variant: pushing the word code
    itself, stop-tagged, without deref — quotation as use/mention at the
    parser level, matching the §6 `descend`/`stop` tags. Because the PS
    side otherwise never contains orthographic content,
    re-symbolization of a reduced idea (ref-search) is guaranteed free
    of form leakage.
  - **Mutex scope (decided default + recorded caveat).** The mutex is the
    **single-writer rule on the workspace**: per serial tick, exactly one
    move (split | shift | reduce) writes. Whether parallel PS synthesis
    continues preattentively beneath serial parsing — with the §3
    truth-measure conflict region (taint) as the preemption signal — is
    left to the grammar-ops corrective pass; serial-*with*-attention and
    its meronymic (CS→PS) and taxonymic (CS→SS) masks are already
    specified in `doc/plans/2026-05-29-stm-serial-parallel-modes.md`, and
    the taxonymic mask is a retrieval-side sibling of the §4 validity
    mask — the corrective pass should unify them.

## 7. The Mind subspace (the region outside space)

- **Definition (rev 2026-06-11).** *Mind* is the region of the meronomy
  whose objects are **wholes of symbols** — σ-tower codes gathering
  symbol-atoms (§6) — concretely marked by their **zeroed positional
  bands**: `.where = 0`, `.when = 0`, unlike any real object. No tag
  is needed: symbols are opaque *by construction* (atoms have no
  insides to inspect), and the zero-band signature distinguishes the
  region with no new wire types. The earlier formulation ("cells whose
  branches stop") is superseded with the tags.
- **Cordon = the table discipline (structural, not checked).** The §1
  incidental/asserted split is the cordon; nothing needs to raise:
  1. **Folds never confer reference-hood.** Only the gate binds a table
     row. A fold result is a fresh vector, not a binding — you cannot
     compute your way to being a name, and downstream semantic use of
     an unbound code fails naturally (there is nothing to look up).
  2. **σ-only composition of symbols.** Symbol-wholes gather (binding,
     aggregation, listing). Semantic π is **delegated downstairs**:
     deref, run π on referent content in the object tower, re-bind the
     parts if licensed. (Concept combination is non-intersective;
     intersecting *symbol codes* is meaningless even though
     intersecting *forms* is well-defined and merely form-level, §1.)
  3. **`part()` is total and means form, everywhere.** Geometric
     relations involving symbol codes are incidental/form-level and
     semantically silent; semantic subsumption is a *different call*
     (deref + dominance, §8). Reading one relation as the other is the
     category error (§1).
  4. **Absence lives here.** "not-A" is a **present** signal — a
     certain, negatively-signed activation (`a = −1`) on A's row —
     gated per §3; never negative mass in ground space.
- **Reading.** Crossing the cordon is exactly the §6 gate (upward:
  search-then-bind) or grounding (downward: deref/decode). The region's
  membership is read off the zero-band signature, not off any stored
  order.

## 8. The reference table as intension store

> **Rev 2026-06-11.** Restated with §6: constructor cells, branch tags,
> and derived order are struck. What this section carried devolves as
> follows — *structure* → the towers (wholes of symbols are σ codes;
> extents/intents are the two codebooks, §5); *the table* → the
> word-keyed binding store (§6); *⊥/⊤ degeneracies* → tower facts
> (below); *subsumption* → the two engines survive, reworded (below);
> *hyperintensionality* → resolved: identity is content +
> participation; no stored derivational identity (below).

- **Records are bindings.** A table row is `(word, object)` — the
  ramsified per-symbol storage, `O(1)` per row; a symbol's entire
  identity is its position (the index is a bound variable; the §6
  symbol code is "approximately" it). The geometry (gauge-oriented
  rows; cached evaluated extents in the two codebooks) is a **cache
  for bound and minted codes**; the term space is generative and never
  enumerated.
- **Shape.** The taxonomy is a doubly-rooted polyhierarchy carried by
  the towers — roots `⊤`/`𝟘` and the atom frontier; multiple parents
  expected (shared parts); acyclic because binding and minting are
  append-only (no recursive definitions in v1; every chain grounds at
  atoms).
- **`⊥` and `⊤` are the two degeneracies**, and both are tower facts,
  not table rows. A minted code whose constraints annihilate evaluates
  to `𝟘` — definable-but-empty; evaluate-before-cache is the
  incoherence detector, and `⊥`-valued codes are the symbolic-level
  home of contradiction (cf. the §3 truth measure's conflict region at
  the activation level). Dually, a σ code driven to `𝟙` **saturates** —
  it gathers everything and distinguishes nothing: definable-but-
  vacuous, a concept that cannot be thought about (§6 hazard note).
- **Two subsumption engines, one fact.** *Asserted*: what the
  predications say — truths recorded against bound rows, the order the
  corpus accumulates (§5 isomorphic pressure). *Synthetic*: per-witness
  dominance on evaluated extents (`Ops.partOf`; box structure as cache
  index). The §4 weight law makes them **agree at mint**:
  `A ⊑ σ(A,B)` and `π(A,B) ⊑ A` are theorems of the geometry the
  moment a whole is minted — so no separate inference-rule engine
  exists. Training drift is the only divergence channel; the
  registration loss (§5) maintains agreement, and residual
  disagreements route as before: containments holding only
  geometrically are candidate definitions (promotable); holding only
  in the asserted order are training signal for registration.
- **Identity is content + participation (resolved 2026-06-11; no
  stored derivational identity).** A code that is the product of σ/π
  over several iterations is identified by its **content** — it is
  content-queried, and content-queried retrieval re-centers it, so it
  stabilizes bottom-up — and its **participation in the conceptual
  space provides the meaning**, as opposed to the encoding process
  that installed it there. The derivation is scaffolding, and the §4
  laws make this lossless where it matters: meaning-relevant
  derivational differences (non-associative composition, attachment)
  surface as *content* differences, so distinct readings stay distinct
  by content alone, while associative re-bracketings — semantically
  equivalent — rightly collapse to one code. Hyperintensional
  distinctions survive exactly where language marks them: distinct
  words are distinct table rows even when bound to the same or
  near-same object ("morning star"/"evening star"). Provenance/replay,
  if ever needed, is an episodic/event concern, outside the table and
  outside identity.

## 9. Out of scope (explicit non-goals for the plan)

- Complex/amplitude (positive-imaginary) encodings of the catuṣkoṭi:
  explored and **rejected** for cost; signed scalar + the measured
  conflict region (§3 rev b) + TruthLayer-internal bivector is the
  decided representation.
- Signed-amplitude "frame-tagged" conflict (the `1−i` orientation degree of
  freedom): deferred; revisit only if WikiOracle frame-contextualized BOTH
  becomes a first-class model citizen.
- Correlational (non-axis-aligned) intents: handle later via a learned
  mixing basis before the dominance test if needed; not in v1.
- Recursive/co-inductive definitions: forbidden in v1 (append-only
  acyclicity).
- **Geometric abstraction classifier** (disconnection over witness dims):
  rejected — no adjacency exists over the dims; σ extents are single lumps
  (§4); class-hood is carried by symbol-wholes in the tower with the
  zero-band signature (§6, rev 2026-06-11).
- **Positional order encodings** (index bands, leading-bit schemes):
  rejected — and (rev 2026-06-11) stored/derived epistemic order is
  struck altogether; nothing encodes order, by index arithmetic or
  otherwise. The symbol code is "approximately the index" for lookup
  convenience only — it carries identity, never order (§6).

## 10. Acceptance criteria (what the plan's tests must show)

1. **Order theorems hold architecturally:** on `PiLayer2`/`SigmaLayer2`
   with random admissible weights, `π(m) ≼ min(operands)` and
   `σ(m) ≽ max(operands)` elementwise; monotone transport up both towers.
2. **Roots behave:** `π(A, 𝟙) = A`, `σ(A, 𝟘) = A`, `π(A, 𝟘) = 𝟘`,
   `σ(A, 𝟙) = 𝟙` (to ε).
3. **Encoding and gauge laws:** relabeling a stored row `u ↦ −u` (with
   activation reorientation) is observationally invariant under
   reference-half lookup; identity = `argmax |q·v|`, polarity =
   `sign(q·v)`; certainty survives retrieval (`(a·u)·v = a(u·v)` to ε);
   ground/token full-vector lookup unaffected.
4. **De Morgan exactness:** `σ = 𝟙 − π(𝟙 − ·)` to numerical tolerance with
   a single shared kernel.
5. **Registration and drift:** `σπσ = σ` loss decreases and the fixed-point
   set is non-degenerate on a toy corpus; after perturbation/training
   steps, minted cells re-register (dominance over their parts restored or
   flagged); asserted pairs verified by dominance.
6. **Interface law:** the percept path writes only `a ∈ [0, +1]`; `a = 0`
   on absence of stimulation; `a < 0` unreachable from the percept path;
   prasajya writes occur only via rival or gated-absence paths (unit tests
   on the gate); evaluation chart exact: `+1 ↦ 1`, `0 ↦ ½`, `−1 ↦ 0`.
7. **Truth measure (rev 2026-06-10b):** `TruthLayer.luminosity` is the
   catuṣkoṭi measure over the stored codes — per conceptual dimension,
   pole coverage `(T_k, F_k)`; result `mean_k[(T_k − F_k) − min(T_k, F_k)]`
   clamped to `[−1, 1]`; computed with **no decode pullback** (a poisoned
   decoder is never called) and **order-independent** (any permutation of
   the record order gives the same value); positive-only stores measure
   positive; sign-opposed evidence on the same dimension subtracts its
   overlap; duplicate truths do not inflate coverage.
8. **Table discipline (rev 2026-06-11):** rows are full only (a row
   exists iff word AND object are bound; partial rows rejected at the
   API; bound-ness is never stored, only discovered by query); the
   table is word-keyed — deref (word → object) is indexed; **no
   object→name index exists anywhere** (API audit: ref is a search of
   the object side; tip-of-the-tongue reproduces as a failed search);
   binding is append-only and only the gate creates rows (folds never
   do); symbol codes are quasi-orthogonal with zeroed `.where`/`.when`
   (pairwise incomparable under dominance — mereologically inert —
   property test); wholes of symbols carry the zero-band signature.
9. **Two engines, one fact:** at mint, asserted order and geometric
   dominance agree on minted wholes (`A ⊑ σ(A,B)`, `π(A,B) ⊑ A`) by
   construction (property test over random admissible weights);
   `⊥`-valued codes are cached as empty and queryable as such;
   ⊤-saturated codes are detectable (the §6 hazard); asserted vs
   synthetic subsumption agree on asserted pairs after registration.
10. **Weight-law parametrization:** on the new layer path, `diag(W) ≥ 1`
    (block-diagonals for binary folds), `offdiag(W) ≥ 0`, `b ≤ 0` hold by
    construction at init and after arbitrary optimizer steps; the `stable`
    clamp bounds `d` in `[1, d_max]`.
11. **Mode coincidence (serial/parallel mutex):** with
    `<conceptualMode>serial</conceptualMode>`, the grammar engages as
    duals — SS analysis by codebook match and binary **split**, PS
    synthesis by binary **reduce** (no whole-slab folds fire); per
    serial tick exactly one move (split | shift | reduce) writes the
    workspace; a shift fires where SS analysis reaches a word in the SS
    codebook and writes the dereferenced **semantic** referent to the
    PS side (the word code only under a stop-tagged mention-shift); the
    reduce trace is recoverable while its events live (no stored
    derivational identity — §8, identity is content + participation);
    for
    associative content the serial reduce chain's extent matches the
    parallel σ extent to tolerance; on the reverse (generation) pass
    the move sets swap — PS split|shift, SS reduce|shift — with
    ref-search supplying the shifted word code; in parallel mode the
    callosum's `2N` mixing matrix carries the per-tick fusion and the
    serial buffers stay untouched.
